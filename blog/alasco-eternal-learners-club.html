For Alasco's bookworms: The Alasco Eternal Learners Club

For almost two years now, every other Tuesday Alascians come together to learn from one another, they meet at the Alasco Eternal Learners Club, short: AELC. Each time a team member presents a book, a framework or even a NGO they found inspiring to share the knowledge with others. Once in a while we have external speakers for the entire team as well.

How did we get the idea?

AELC was founded in 2019 by two of our amazing team members - Kevin and Hanna. Both of them have been using their yearly Development Budget to buy multiple books about Management, Leadership or other topics related to their work. They realized that both had bought the same book and came up with the idea to create a platform that allows them to share what they’ve read with other Alscians to inspire them as well. That was the start for the bi-weekly book club that has been going on ever since.

Joining AELC or presenting a book is absolutely voluntary. Each Alascian has the chance to sign up for a session. They then prepare a short presentation about the author and the main takeaways from the book and then discuss their learnings. Not seldom the presentation ends in a discussion of all participants about their opinions towards the book.

Why share knowledge?

It is known that sharing knowledge among groups (in this case colleagues) has a great positive effect. As mentioned in the beginning, a lot of Alasicans buy books from their yearly development budget to dive deeper into certain topics of their work. We found that there are also common interests among different teams such as time management, emotional intelligence or setting goals.

With AELC we want to bring those who have the same interest together and stimulate the exchange between them. This does not only foster a closer relationship between colleagues, but also supports a constant knowledge sharing between them.

Further we want to create a learning culture where everyone not only feels safe to share their knowledge but more importantly to ask colleagues that might have more experience or expertise in a topic. AELC is a great way to support that.

What has been presented?

The topics presented and discussed reach from philosophical topics such as “Confucius” up to more work related AELC sessions regarding Deep work or Design Thinking:

  • The big five for life
  • Factfulness
  • Analects - 孔夫子 Confucius
  • Die Kunst ein kreatives Leben zu führen
  • Reicher als die Geissens
  • Design Thinking
  • Atomic Habits
  • No Excuses!: The Power of Self-Discipline
  • The Elephants - A system for better living
  • Why we sleep
  • Memory Book - Hacks for better memory
  • The subtle art of not giving a fuck
  • Emotional Intelligence
  • Emotional Agility
  • Deep work
  • Chris Voss
  • Louder than words - Joe Navarro
  • Aim for growth - Gerd Kommer AELC Masterclass
  • Introduction to Scrum
  • Achtsam Morden
  • Invisible Women. Exposing data in a world designed for men/li>

  • Thinking, Fast and Slow
