Teamevents 2020

We love to organize regular team events to bring everyone together and to strengthen the bond within the team. Last year was certainly a special one, we had many great events planned, but due to the Covid pandemic we had to cancel some of them and come up with new - remote -ideas. We certainly came up with some cool events that we could have remotely or in person and wanted to share what to inspire other HR teams as well. Therefore we will leave out the non-remote events such as our Beer Pong tournament in January or our skiing trip.

Beer tasting

Support your local meets team building! In April we organized a remote beer tasting, collaborating with Alexander Schönbach from Hoppebräu. He introduced us to the art of brewing. The icing on the cake was the joint virtual beer tasting of the 'handgmachtn Kraftbiere' (handmade craft beer) from Bad Tölz.

Exit the room

Our next team event was a bit more adventurous and collaborative. Our Alascians went on a rally, working through a mysterious case in an online Exit The Room game. To make it easier to talk to each other, we split the team into groups of 3 to 5 people, each in their own break out room. It stays a mystery which team was the fastest, opinions differ on that matter ;-)

Birthday/Series A dinner

In June we faced a challenge: How can you celebrate your birthday and have a closing dinner without neglecting official Corona guidelines? Our solution: You invite everyone for a cozy remote dinner. We ordered food from Goldmarie here in Munich. They deliver the different courses pre-prepared in glasses, which makes it easy for everyone to heat the food and enjoy the time with the colleagues.

Crime rally

In July the regulations were a little bit less strict and we were able to organize a city crime rallye through Munich for all those who felt safe enough to join. Equipped with Ipads we went throughout Schwabing to collect evidence, talk to witnesses and to identify the murderer. After such a long period working from home, the event really felt like a relief to everyone. Thanks to Evinta for the great experience!

Ice cream cart

Too hot to handle? August came with a lot of sun and above average temperatures. What better way to cool off than to organize a mobile ice cream cart? Werner from Carrettino took his spot in our parking lot and delighted our Alascians with delicious ice cream. From chocolate, vanilla and cassis… a well deserved break during these hot temperatures.

Hiking

Unofficial but yet a fun team event: Hiking with your colleagues. For August we decided to have a non-official team event and invited some Alascians to go on a hike together. 10 sporty team members took the train from Munich to lake Schliersee. From there they walked to lake Tegernsee via Prinzenweg. With a great view from the top of the mountains they enjoyed their lunch breakwith some Brotzeit, happy about quality time with colleagues and being outside in nature.

Weißwurst Frühstück

End of September in Munich means: Time for the Oktoberfest, an event that many Alascians were looking forward to. Sadly, earlier this summer the Munich officials decided to cancel the Oktoberfest for 2020. To overcome some of the sadness we tried to organize an alternative and organized a Weißwurstfrühstück in the office instead. We served the traditional food (with sausages from Magnus Bauch) and provided (alcohol free) beer for everyone. We all dressed up in the traditional costumes, listened to bavarian music while eating delicious Weißwurst with bretzels whilst keeping the distance to each other.

Christmas Party

Bringing the year to a festive end we usually celebrate a big Christmas Party. To duplicate that, we organized a remote dinner with games, different breakout sessions and a distance disco that lasted until late that night. Starting with a christmas poem by our P&O team, the team went into different breakout rooms, sharing each course with different team mates. After the quiz from our Founder Team we unpacked the christmas presents that we had sent out previously. The presents represented our five values: Alasco Sweatpants (frontrunner), a Notebook (Do it), 80% Rum (80% Consens = 100% Commitment), a tiny sunflower (Aim for growth) & a colleague appreciation postcard (be “nett”) that the Alascians could send to a fellow colleague. After dinner we moved into the party area to keep celebrating, e.g. dancing in distance disco, chatting at the bonfire, playing truth or dare at the cocktail bar.

What’s next?

Despite the circumstances, 2020 was full of fun and successful team events. For the new year, we decided to go with smaller initiatives to keep the team connected and in a good spirit. We organize Lunch and Coffee Roulettes, connect Alascians for Walk and Talk Meetings and in March we are excited to go on a remote skiing trip with everyone.
