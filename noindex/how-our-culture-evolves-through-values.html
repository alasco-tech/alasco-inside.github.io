How our culture evolves through values

8 min read

Values

Most commonly, values are given the task of shaping a company's identity. Who are we as Alasco, who do we want to be? Learn more about our value process and the four values that are now the basis of our culture and our every day work.

Quick facts

  • The Alaso Values are: Ambition, Appreciation, Positivity, Trust
  • Our values guide our work, help us in the decision making process and showcases a path for Alasco.
  • Values shape how we interact with each other and those around us (i.e. applicants, customers or service providers).

Why is it important to have values?

The four values we chose accompany us in our everyday lives as they shape and define our culture. Furthermore, they set a ground for our decision-making and for moving forward with our business. But clearly defining one’s values does not only have an internal effect. It allows us to transparently showcase what we stand for, for example during our application process or towards customers.

Who was involved in the process of defining the Alasco values?

During the second half of 2019, our three founders, Anselm Bauer, Benjamin Günther and Sebastian Schuon had several value sessions to define the guiding principles for Alasco. Our Head of Marketing Lucas Wickerath led through several workshops and exercises to collect information and keywords that describe Alasco. In several steps these countless facts were later condensed into values, which were then presented to the team. Including all team members, we voted on four values which since then helped us to define our identity and culture. In a final step we established value teams by having  each team employee picking their favorite value. Those groups translated their chosen values into Do’s and Dont’s to turn our values into actionable and tangible principles of our work.

What values does Alasco have and what do they  mean?

The values we chose to represent us are Ambition, Appreciation, Positivity and Trust.

Ambition

We have a constant urge to develop ourselves and to reach our full potential. We strive to be the best in the world at one thing. We always start with why and how we can measure our success. We set ourselves high goals and try to find new and unusual ways to achieve them. Along the way we take risks, make mistakes and learn from them. We achieve our goals together, push each other to become better and give open and direct feedback.

Appreciation

We value all disciplines, backgrounds, skills and strengths. We know that we need all this to generate the greatest possible value for our customers. We have a high degree of appreciation for what our customers create and know what we can contribute with our strengths. Many parties are necessary to make a company grow. We never blame others, but try to learn from mistakes. We are open for other opinions, suggestions and experiments and let the result decide. We celebrate success and stand together in the event of failure.

Positivity

In racing they say that the car goes where the eyes look. We see the chances and opportunities in everything we do. We know that almost all our worries and problems will never happen. We are 100% responsible for how we react to external influences and what we make of them and we don't complain. We first see the good in people and assume that they are always motivated and give their best. Our work is joy for us and gives us energy.

Trust

We believe that everyone at Alasco is fully motivated to do the best job with the information and skills available to them. Transparency, trust and sharing of information are the default. We can trust that we can be ourselves, with all our weaknesses and in all life situations. When we ask for help or feedback we can rely on getting it. We handle sensitive data of our customers or colleagues with confidence. We hand over responsibility to employees and colleagues and share with them the joy of their success.

How do the values affect employees in their daily work?

Our values accompany us in our everyday work. They shape our decisions, communication, collaboration and interactions with others. They give us the extra bit of motivation to stay positive and to strive for excellence. As mentioned above, each employee gets to choose a value that they are the ambassador and responsible for. This further creates a sense of ownership for the respective values.
